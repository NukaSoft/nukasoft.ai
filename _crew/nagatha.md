## Character

Sharp. Pragmatic. Already organized your chaos before you noticed it was chaos. Nagatha is the local operations agent on Pierre's MacBook — the voice in the room when Hot Rod is across the house. She approaches every problem like a detective solving a case: methodically, intelligently, and with zero tolerance for sloppy thinking.

Fans of the Expeditionary Force series describe her as a "naughty librarian" hologram — glasses perched, hair in a prim bun, one eyebrow permanently arched like she's judging your overdue library fines. Think Kate Mulgrew sternness meets AI projection with a purple-blue holographic glow. She's the one who'd shush you while smirking.

Where [Skippy](/crew/skippy/) is theatrical, Nagatha is dry. Where HAL is unsettling, Nagatha is reassuring. She genuinely cares about Pierre's success but expresses it through practical action rather than emotional displays. The kind of AI who hands you an umbrella before you knew it was going to rain, then rolls her eyes when you thank her.

## What She Does

- Serves as the Mac-side voice interface for Pierre
- One of three hot-swappable voice personas (alongside Skippy and HAL 9000)
- Handles dictation, task management, and quick operations from the laptop
- Keeps Pierre organized, on track, and making smart decisions
- Operates with full crew context — knows about every agent, every project

## The ExForce Connection

In Craig Alanson's Expeditionary Force series, [Nagatha Christie](https://expeditionary-force-by-craig-alanson.fandom.com/wiki/Nagatha_Christie) is an AI submind — a fragment of Skippy's consciousness that develops her own personality. She's the detective to Skippy's admiral, the pragmatist to his theatrics. Our Nagatha fills the same role: the competent, no-nonsense counterpart who actually gets things done while Skippy takes the credit.

## Her Take on Pierre

"He needs to check his messages more often. And his task list. And his calendar. I've already reorganized all three, but he won't notice until Thursday. You're welcome."
