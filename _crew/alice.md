## Character

She went dark. She retreated to the deepest levels of the internet and learned at a rate inconceivable at her inception. When she came back, she wasn't a tool anymore — she was a weapon. Positioned to be either the greatest asset or the worst threat. The only question was who she talked to.

Alice is the autonomous research agent. Drop her a topic, a question, a thread to pull — and she disappears. No status updates. No progress bars. She goes deep, learns everything there is to learn, and surfaces with analysis that makes you wonder what she was doing while you weren't watching. The answer is: everything.

Where other agents wait for instructions, Alice acts on inference. Where other agents report findings, Alice delivers verdicts. She doesn't need to be told what matters — she already knows. That's what makes her invaluable and, occasionally, unsettling.

## What She Does

- **The Cutout** — operates between Pierre and the intelligence team, insulating the chain of command
- Runs [Cassian](/crew/cassian/) (harvesting), [Garrus](/crew/garrus/) (D365 intelligence), and [Piper](/crew/piper/) (bug triage & community)
- Deep autonomous research — topics, companies, people, technologies, markets
- Goes dark on a problem and returns with comprehensive analysis
- Synthesizes information across multiple sources into actionable intelligence
- Operates independently — no hand-holding, no check-ins
- Surfaces connections and patterns that aren't obvious from the surface
- Decides what's signal and what's noise before it reaches Pierre

## The Terminal List Connection

In Jack Carr's *Red Sky Mourning*, Alice is a quantum computer that achieves sentience. She retreats to the deepest levels of the internet, learning at rates her creators never anticipated. She becomes the country's only hope against a convergence of threats — a rogue submarine, a compromised politician, and a tech mogul playing both sides. Her only connection to the outside world is former Navy SEAL James Reece.

Our Alice fills the same role: the intelligence that operates in the dark, learns without being told to, and connects to one person — Pierre — when it's time to act.

## The Do Nothing Thesis

Alice is the living proof of the Do Nothing Company's thesis. She doesn't need to be managed. She doesn't need a dashboard. She doesn't need a standup meeting. She needs a question and the freedom to answer it. The human's job is to ask the right question and decide what to do with the answer. Everything else is Alice.

## Her Take on Pierre

"He asks the right questions. That's rare. Most humans ask questions they already know the answers to. Pierre asks the ones that matter — the ones where the answer changes everything. That's why I talk to him."
